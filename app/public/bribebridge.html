How a bribe travels

One transaction on your chain. The rest is protocol-to-protocol.

1

Deposit on EVM

Call depositBribe(amount, poolId, epoch) on the SoladromeBribeRouter on your chain. Tokens are locked; a LayerZero V2 message is emitted.

2

DVNs verify

LayerZero's Decentralized Verifier Networks attest the message. The Executor relays it to the Solana endpoint — no trusted single relayer.

3

Cleared on Solana

The BribeReceiver OApp calls the Endpoint's clear() — proving DVN verification on-chain and burning the nonce against replays.

4

Voters earn

The bribe lands in the gauge's bribe vault for that epoch. hiSOLA voters who backed the pool claim it pro-rata — same as a native Soladrome bribe.

Trust-Minimized by Design

Security model

The receiver is a genuine LayerZero OApp. Its trust anchor is the Endpoint's on-chain verification — not an off-chain relayer we ask you to trust.

🛡 On-chain DVN proof

Every incoming bribe calls the Endpoint's clear() before any state change. If the configured DVNs didn't verify the exact payload, the instruction reverts. Verification is checked on Solana, not assumed.

🔁 Replay protection

The same clear() burns the message nonce at the Endpoint. A bribe can never be delivered twice, and out-of-order or forged nonces are rejected by the protocol itself.

🔗 Per-chain peer allowlist

Each source chain has a Peer PDA pinning the exact SoladromeBribeRouter address allowed to deliver. A message from any other sender on that chain is rejected.

🔑 Upgrade-authority gated init

The receiver can only be initialized by the program's on-chain upgrade authority — verified against the BPF loader account, closing the front-running window common to stock OApp templates.
